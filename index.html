About

I'm a Computer Science and Engineering graduate from North South University (CGPA 3.09/4.00, May 2026), currently working as an undergraduate researcher at the Apurba–NSU R&D Lab. My work centers on multivariate time series forecasting: designing architectures that model cross-variate dependencies and long-range temporal patterns without the quadratic cost of attention.

My co-developed model, CARNet, was accepted at ECML PKDD 2026 and received reviewer feedback describing it as a possible best-paper candidate. I've also published first-author work on Mamba-augmented Transformers for financial forecasting, and I compete regularly in ICPC and other programming contests — a habit that keeps my implementation and algorithmic thinking sharp alongside the research.

I'm now looking toward Master's or PhD where I can push further on efficient sequence architectures and foundation models for time series.

CARNet: Cycle-Aware Core Aggregation Network for Multivariate Time Series Forecasting

Shuvo, M. S. S. — Second Author

Co-developed a cycle-aware core aggregation framework with an efficient core redistribution mechanism for multivariate forecasting, evaluated on five benchmarks (ETT, Electricity, Traffic, Weather, PEMS). A reviewer described it as a possible best-paper candidate.

How Effective is Mamba-Augmented Transformer for Stock Market Forecasting?

Shuvo, M. S. S. — First Author

Proposed CrossMamba, a causal Mamba-based architecture for efficient cross-sequence interaction in stock market forecasting, achieving competitive results against Transformer- and Mamba-based baselines on S&P 500 and Yahoo Finance data.
